Privacy Policy

How we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

Home/Privacy Policy

Last Updated: January 1, 2024

Matshona Attorneys ("we", "our", "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your personal information when you visit our website or engage our legal services.

This policy is compliant with the Protection of Personal Information Act, 2013 (POPIA), the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) where applicable.

1. Information We Collect

Personal Information You Provide

  • Full name, email address, phone number
  • Physical and/or postal address
  • Details of your legal matter (via contact forms or chatbot)
  • Any documents or information you share with us

Information Collected Automatically

  • IP address and browser type
  • Pages visited and time spent on our website
  • Cookies and similar tracking technologies
  • Device information and operating system

2. How We Use Your Information

  • To respond to your enquiries and provide legal services
  • To schedule consultations and manage your case
  • To improve our website and user experience
  • To send relevant legal updates (with your consent)
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations

3. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal information based on: your consent, the necessity to perform a contract, compliance with legal obligations, and our legitimate interests in providing and improving our services.

4. Data Sharing & Third Parties

We do not sell your personal information. We may share your data with:

  • Trusted service providers (hosting, email, CRM systems) under strict data processing agreements
  • Courts, regulatory bodies, or law enforcement when required by law
  • Third-party integrations you interact with (e.g., Calendly for scheduling)

5. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information, including encryption (HTTPS/TLS), access controls, and secure data storage. However, no method of electronic transmission is 100% secure.

6. Your Rights

Under POPIA and GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access your personal information we hold
  • Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Object to or restrict processing of your data
  • Data portability — receive your data in a structured format
  • Withdraw consent at any time
  • Lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator (South Africa)

7. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to enhance your browsing experience. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings. Essential cookies are required for the website to function properly. Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors use our site.

9. Data Retention

We retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes outlined in this policy, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. Legal case files may be retained for a minimum of 5 years as required by the Legal Practice Act.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated revision date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
